Retirement Calculator

A generic retirement calculator to help you figure out how much you'll have when you retire.

How it works

The calculator uses the value for your contributions towards saving to determine the living costs: living costs = salary * (100% - contributions). From there for each year the salary is increased by the specified factor and the living costs by inflation value.

The yearly savings are calculated by subtracting the living costs from the salary, they are then added to the total savings. We multiply each years total by the annual rate of return. To be more specific, the total savings you have access to for any given year is: (previous year's total savings) * rate of return + current years savings. This is similar to how a savings account usually works and to simplify we also apply the same logic to investments.

Once the living costs outpace the income, we start using up our savings. For that we use the value of the tax to calculate how much we need to withdraw/sell (the gross value) in order to have an amount that is equal the deficit for that year.
